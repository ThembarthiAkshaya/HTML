  1. Marinate the chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, salt, and lemon juice. Let it sit for at least 1 hour.
  2. Wash and soak basmati rice for 30 minutes.
  3. Fry sliced onions in oil until golden brown and crispy. Set aside some for garnishing.
  4. Parboil the rice in salted water with a bay leaf, cloves, and cardamom. Drain when 70% cooked.
  5. Layer marinated chicken at the bottom of a thick-bottomed pan (or handi).
  6. Add a layer of rice over the chicken.
  7. Sprinkle fried onions, chopped mint, and coriander leaves over the rice.
  8. Drizzle saffron milk or yellow food color and a little ghee over the top.
  9. Seal the pot with a lid and dough or foil, then cook on low heat (dum) for 30 to 40 minutes.
  10. Let it rest for 10 minutes, then gently mix before serving hot with raita or salan.
